Dinner,
for one.

Cook what's already in your fridge. No families. No meal-prep for twelve. Just dinner, for you.

Cooking apps are built for families. This one isn't.

The average UK solo dweller spends over £200 a month on takeaway. A week of home-cooked meals costs £30. The maths isn't close. SoloCook tracks every meal you cook and tells you, in plain pounds, what you've saved.
What's in the app

Three doors, one dinner.

1

The fridge filter.

Tap the ingredients you have. See the 115,000-recipe catalogue narrow down to what you can cook right now, plus what you're one ingredient short on.

2

Scales for one.

Every recipe flips between "just me" and "me + one" with a single tap. Quantities, kcal, and time adjust. No wasted food, no leftovers that haunt your fridge for four days.

3

A quiet streak.

Every meal you cook stamps a takeaway's worth into a running counter on Home. Share an editorial card to Stories when you feel quietly smug.

Quietly, by default

No account. No server.

SoloCook runs entirely on your phone. No analytics. No login. No friends list. No pings. If you delete the app, everything's gone.

  • All state stored on-device. Nothing sent to us, because there is no us.
  • No tracking, no advertising, no data shared with third parties.
  • Network requests are limited to anonymous image and recipe lookups.
  • Your data leaves your device only when you press share.
